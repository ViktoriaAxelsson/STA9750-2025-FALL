  1. The movie Red Notice has a runtime of 1 hour and 58 minutes. Approximately how many views did it receive in 2021? Note that Netflix does not provide the weekly_views values that far back in the past, but you can compute it yourself using the total view time and the runtime.
Code 
Rednotice_views <- GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  filter(show_title=="Red Notice")|> 
  summarise(
    total_hours = sum(weekly_hours_viewed, na.rm=TRUE),
    runtime_hours = 1+58/60, 
    total_views=total_hours /runtime_hours,
    .groups = "drop")
  • The Red Notice received 230,842,373 views in 2021.


  1. How many Films reached Number 1 in the US but did not originally debut there? That is, find films that first appeared on the Top 10 chart at, e.g., Number 4 but then became more popular and eventually hit Number 1? What is the most recent film to pull this off?
Code 
films_US <- COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  filter(category=="Films", country_name=="United States")

debut_ranks <-films_US |> 
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(
    debut_week = min(week), 
    debut_rank = weekly_rank[week==debut_week],
    .groups = "drop"
  )

top_one  <- films_US|> 
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(
    number_one=any(weekly_rank==1), 
    last_week = max(week),
    .groups="drop"
  )

film_not_debut_one = debut_ranks|> 
  inner_join(top_one, by = "show_title") |> 
  filter(number_one,debut_rank > 1) 


most_recent = film_not_debut_one|> 
  arrange(desc(last_week)) |> 
  slice(1)

  • A total of 45 appeared on the Top 10 chart and then moved to top 1.

  • The most recent was KPop Demon Hunters.

Netflix has a significant presence in Scandinavia, having launched in the region in 2012 and opening its first Nordic hub in Stockholm in 2021. There are many growth opportunities to explore in this region of the world to maximise profits.

  • In Sweden, Netflix has one of the highest streaming rates globally, with a significant daily reach.

  • Netflix has increased its focus on Nordic productions, launching a slate of original films and TV shows by expanding its local content from 2018 onwards.

  • One of the Top Scandinavian shows Secrets We Keep, was released May 15 2025 worldwide. Not only has it been popular in Scandinavia, but it’s had a total weekly average viewership around the world of: 6,380,000 since its release.
